Teaching notes:

  • Calculating pairs well requires understanding the dictionary qualities of objects.
  • Calculating three of a kind, if done after pairs, may not work, so they have to think about ordering.
  • When going from calculating pairs to three-of-a-kind, you have a good example of refactoring.
  • Flush can be a good example of an early exit, also looping starting at second element.
  • Straight requires sorting, thinking of face cards as numeric sequencies, looping while always looking at previous.
  • Straight flush requires combining both the flush and straight functions, and then ordering that before both of them.
